Pet-Friendly Restaurants and Bars in the Tampa Bay Area

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

The best vacation rentals allow dogs. The best trips allow dogs everywhere. Tampa Bay is genuinely one of the more dog-welcoming metro areas in Florida, with a solid lineup of restaurants, bars, and waterfront spots that don't just tolerate your dog — they actually seem happy to see them.

Starting your trip right: Sea & City Rentals pet-friendly properties put you close to all of the spots below. Let us know about your dog when you book.

St. Petersburg

Green Bench Brewing Company

The outdoor space at Green Bench is massive, well-shaded, and explicitly dog-friendly. Excellent rotating tap list, good food (smoked meats, sandwiches, pretzels), and the vibe is exactly what you want from a brewery on a Saturday afternoon. Your dog will meet approximately 11 other dogs. They will all be happy.

Locality Beer

Smaller and more neighborhood in feel, Locality Beer has a covered patio and a genuinely welcoming attitude toward dogs. Good tap list focused on approachable styles done well.

Trophy Fish

Waterfront, covered patio, dog-friendly. A seafood and cocktail spot on the south St. Pete waterfront with great views of Tampa Bay and a layout that works well for dog owners.

Clearwater & Clearwater Beach

Island Way Grill

Waterfront dining on the Intracoastal with a dog-friendly patio. The seafood is fresh and seriously prepared. The view of the marina and the sunset makes it a go-to for a dog-friendly dinner that doesn't compromise on the meal.

Clear Sky Cafe

One of the most reliably dog-friendly spots on Clearwater Beach, with outdoor seating right on the beach strip. Great for a casual lunch. The grouper sandwich is a staple.

Indian Rocks Beach & Largo

Tom's Place Waterfront Bar

On the Intracoastal side of Indian Rocks Beach, Tom's Place has dock seating and a laid-back atmosphere that's perfect for dogs. Cold beer, real views, dogs genuinely welcome.

Crabby Bill's

The original Indian Rocks location has covered outdoor seating where dogs are welcome. Fresh Gulf seafood, no fuss, the right setting for a dog-friendly seafood lunch.

Tips for Dining Out with Your Dog

  • Call ahead even when a place is listed as dog-friendly — policies change and patio capacity varies
  • Bring a portable water bowl — not every restaurant will think to offer one
  • Avoid peak weekend lunch hours if your dog is reactive to crowds and noise
  • Keep your dog on a short leash and close to your chair
  • Tip well when you bring a dog — managing dog-adjacent tables takes extra effort
