Largo and Seminole: Hidden Gems Just Off the Beach

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

Largo and Seminole don't appear on many travel itineraries, and that's precisely what makes them worth knowing about. Sitting in the middle of Pinellas County, they offer a quieter, more residential slice of Gulf Coast Florida that has its own genuine character.

Largo Central Park & Nature Preserve

One of the most underused green spaces in Pinellas County. Largo Central Park surrounds an 80-acre lake with walking trails, picnic pavilions, and a bandshell that hosts free concerts on Friday evenings during warmer months. The nature preserve section has boardwalks through wetland habitats with excellent bird watching — wood storks, great blue herons, and roseate spoonbills are common. The playground is excellent for families, and the lake has a fishing pier.

Pinellas Trail

The 38-mile Pinellas Trail runs directly through Largo and Seminole, connecting to Clearwater to the north and St. Pete to the south. The Largo stretch passes through neighborhoods, past parks, and through areas that give you a real sense of how locals actually live on this peninsula. Bike rentals are available along the trail corridor.

John's Pass Village — Nearby Madeira Beach

Just south of the Largo/Seminole area, John's Pass Village in Madeira Beach is worth including as a half-day destination. A boardwalk fishing village on the Intracoastal with seafood restaurants, shops, dolphin and sunset cruise departures, and working fishing boats. The seafood here is legitimately fresh — several restaurants are steps from the fishing docks. Friendly Fisherman and Mema's Chowder House are local favorites.

Seminole City Center Area

The Seminole area has a solid concentration of local restaurants and casual dining — Florida sports bars, family Mexican restaurants, Thai spots that have been in the same location for a decade. The kind of places locals go when they don't want to drive to St. Pete. Seminole City Center also has a good mix of national retailers alongside local businesses.

Why Largo & Seminole Work Well as a Base

Properties in Largo and Seminole tend to offer more space for the price than beach-adjacent rentals, with easy access to both the Gulf beaches (15–20 minutes to the sand) and the broader Tampa Bay area. For families or groups who want a home base that isn't directly on the tourist corridor, this is a smart choice.
