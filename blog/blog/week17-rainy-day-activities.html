10 Things to Do in Tampa Bay on a Rainy Day

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

The afternoon storm has rolled in, the beach is temporarily off the table, and someone in your group is about to say 'so what do we do now?' Here's the answer. Tampa Bay has a genuinely solid lineup of indoor and covered activities that make rainy afternoons feel like part of the plan rather than a disruption to it.

Rainy afternoons are temporary — storms typically clear in 60–90 minutes. Book a property with a covered lanai at seaandcityrentals.com and watch the storm from there with a cold drink before heading back to the beach.

1. The Salvador Dali Museum — St. Pete

One of the great art museums in the South. The building itself — designed with a flowing geodesic glass structure — is remarkable. Inside, one of the largest collections of Dali's work outside of Spain. Budget two hours minimum. The gift shop is excellent.

2. Clearwater Marine Aquarium

If you haven't been and the weather is driving you indoors, this is the most obvious call. A genuine marine rescue facility, not just an aquarium — the dolphins and sea turtles here are resident animals that couldn't survive in the wild. Educational, moving, and well worth the afternoon.

3. Bowling at Kings — St. Pete or Tampa

Kings Dining & Entertainment has locations in both St. Pete and Tampa — upscale bowling alleys with a full restaurant and bar, arcade games, and billiards. The kind of place where a 2pm rainy afternoon turns into a surprisingly good time. Book ahead on weekends.

4. Escape Rooms

The Tampa Bay area has a solid escape room scene. Escape Countdown in St. Pete and Trapped Escape Room in Tampa are both well-reviewed. Good for groups, teens who've run out of beach energy, or couples who want a competitive afternoon. Most rooms accommodate 4–8 people and run about an hour.

5. Museum of Science & Industry (MOSI) — Tampa

Tampa's science museum has exhibits ranging from a butterfly garden to a hurricane simulator to a planetarium. Great for families when the beach isn't an option. The IMAX theater is worth checking what's showing.

6. Locale Market — St. Pete

A long, leisurely lunch at Locale Market during a rainstorm is genuinely enjoyable. The food hall format means everyone picks exactly what they want, the quality is consistently high, and there's no reason to rush. Order wine and wait out the storm in comfort.

7. Craft Brewery Afternoon

St. Pete has an impressive craft beer scene, and a rainy afternoon is the ideal time to explore it. Green Bench Brewing, Brew Bus Brewing, and Cycle Brewing are all within a few miles of downtown. Pick two or three, take an Uber, and make an afternoon of it.

8. Florida Museum of Photographic Arts — Tampa

Rotating exhibitions of photography from regional and national artists in a beautifully converted space in Tampa's Cube building. Smaller and more intimate — good for a focused hour and a half.

9. Largo Indoor Aquatic Center

If you have kids who absolutely must be in water, the Largo indoor aquatic center gives you a pool day regardless of weather. For families with young children who need to burn energy, it works.

10. Movie Afternoon

Sometimes the right call is a good film, a dark theater, and popcorn. CMX Cinemas Countryside in Clearwater and the Regal Starlight in St. Pete are good options. Treat it like part of the vacation — not a consolation prize.
