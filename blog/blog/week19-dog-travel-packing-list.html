How to Travel with Your Dog: A Gulf Coast Packing List

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

Bringing your dog on a Gulf Coast trip is one of the better decisions you can make. The Tampa Bay area has dog-friendly beaches, outdoor restaurants, parks, and vacation rentals that actually welcome your dog rather than just tolerating them. But packing right makes the difference between a smooth trip and a chaotic one.

Before you pack, find the right property. Sea & City Rentals pet-friendly properties — we're happy to answer questions about specific fencing and yard situations when you book.

The Non-Negotiables

  • Collapsible water bowl and extra water — dogs dehydrate fast in Florida heat, and beach water is salt water
  • Leash plus a backup leash — you will lose one somehow
  • Current ID tag with your cell number — not just their home address
  • Health records and vaccination proof — required for some dog beaches and boarding facilities
  • Flea and tick prevention — Florida has year-round exposure
  • Poop bags — more than you think you'll need

Beach-Specific Gear

  • Dog-safe sunscreen for short-haired dogs — noses and ears are most vulnerable
  • Dog booties or paw wax — Gulf sand gets extremely hot midday
  • Shade canopy or umbrella big enough for them to lie under
  • A dedicated dog towel (or three) — rinsing after saltwater is important
  • Small portable fan for under the canopy shade

Comfort & Routine Items

  • Their regular food — changing food during travel is a recipe for GI issues
  • Their bed or a familiar blanket — helps them settle in a new space
  • A few favorite toys — for the vacation rental downtime
  • Any medications they're on, plus veterinarian contact info

For the Car Ride

  • A crash-tested travel crate or dog seatbelt harness — Florida law and basic safety
  • Puppy pads under their crate or bed — just in case
  • A window sun shade for their side
  • Frozen Kong or long-lasting chew for the drive

When You Arrive

Walk the property before you let your dog off-leash inside. Check for fencing gaps, anything toxic in the yard, and confirm the pool is secured if it's present. Your dog is going to love the Gulf Coast.
