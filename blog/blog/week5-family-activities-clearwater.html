Best Family-Friendly Activities Near Clearwater Beach

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

Clearwater Beach is one of those rare family vacation spots that actually delivers on its promise. The beach is stunning, yes — but what keeps families coming back year after year is everything else: the marine life, the sunsets, the activities that give kids something to talk about when school starts again.

Clearwater Marine Aquarium

This is the one that appears on every kid's must-do list. The Clearwater Marine Aquarium is home to rescued marine animals — dolphins, sea turtles, otters, and more — being rehabilitated or who have made the aquarium their permanent home. Budget 2–3 hours, go early to beat the heat and the crowds, and buy tickets in advance online. Kids leave knowing things about sea turtles they'll repeat for months.

Pier 60 — Sunset Every Night

Pier 60 is Clearwater's famous fishing pier that doubles as the city's nightly festival. Sunsets at Pier 60 runs every evening from roughly two hours before sunset to two hours after, with artisan vendors, street performers, and collective sunset-watching that's hard to replicate anywhere else. For kids: fire spinners, musicians, acrobats. For parents: wine and cold drinks nearby.

Caladesi Island State Park

If you want to show your kids what Florida looked like before the hotels showed up, Caladesi Island is it. Only accessible by ferry from Honeymoon Island — which is part of the adventure. The beach is pristine, the kayak trail through the mangroves is extraordinary, and the lack of development means your kids can actually explore without structure. Pack lunch, bring water, and go.

Captain Memo's Pirate Cruise

If your kids are under 12, this is a must. Two-hour pirate-themed cruises out of Clearwater Beach Marina — the crew gets the kids involved in battles, there's water guns, treasure, and the whole production is genuinely fun. Adults get rum punch. Everyone wins. Book ahead — this sells out on summer weekends.

Splash Harbour Water Park — Indian Rocks Beach

On a scorching July afternoon when everyone's had enough sand, Splash Harbour Water Park is the answer. Compact, well-priced compared to larger parks, and has enough slides and splash zones to keep kids busy for hours.

Tips for Families Visiting Clearwater Beach

  • Plan beach time for early morning (9–11am) or late afternoon (4–6pm) — midday Florida sun is brutal for little ones
  • Pack a cooler — beachside concessions are expensive and the lines are long
  • Many restaurants have kids menus and will accommodate early dinner — go at 5pm and skip the wait
  • Vacation rentals with a private pool are a lifesaver when afternoon storms roll in
