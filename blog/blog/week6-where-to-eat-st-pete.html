Where to Eat in St. Pete: Local Favorites by Neighborhood

Sea & City Rentals  ·  Tampa Bay, FL

St. Petersburg has quietly become one of the best food cities in Florida — in the way where locals know it, visiting chefs talk about it, and anyone who eats their way through a weekend here leaves wondering why it took them so long to discover it.

Downtown St. Pete: The Waterfront & Central Ave

The Avenue

New American cuisine with a creative cocktail program that punches above its weight. The menu changes seasonally and the service is genuinely warm. Make a reservation for Friday or Saturday dinner — the bar fills up fast.

Locale Market

The play for groups with different tastes. A full-service food hall with multiple chef-driven counters — pasta, seafood, charcuterie, wood-fired meats. Great for families too.

Sea Salt

The splurge option, and it earns it. Chef Roger Radwan's seafood-forward menu is consistently excellent, the room is beautiful, and the wine list is serious without being intimidating.

Intermezzo Coffee & Cocktails

The anchor of morning and late night in downtown St. Pete. Excellent espresso, beautiful space, and a cocktail menu at night that makes it worth returning to after dinner.

Grand Central District

Burg

Iconic St. Pete burger spot. Smash-style, properly seasoned, unpretentious. The craft beer list changes regularly and the fries are some of the best in the city.

Il Ritorno

Neighborhood Italian that understands what neighborhood Italian should be: handmade pasta, approachable wine, a warm room, food that makes you want to come back the next night.

Mandarin Hide

Cocktail bar first, food second — but both are excellent. One of the most creative cocktail programs in St. Pete, with genuinely good bar snacks. Good spot for a late first drink of the night.

The Edge District

Green Bench Brewing

The go-to craft brewery for St. Pete locals. Excellent beer across a wide range of styles, huge outdoor dog-friendly space, and solid food. Perfect for a laid-back afternoon.

St. Pete Beach Area

Crabby Bill's

A Gulf Coast institution. Fresh grouper, stone crab, shrimp, and oysters — casual, waterfront, unpretentious. The grouper sandwich alone is worth the visit.

Mastry's Brewing Co.

Right on Corey Avenue in St. Pete Beach. Excellent tap list and a kitchen that goes beyond bar food. The brisket tacos and smash burgers are regulars on the menu.

Make reservations Thursday before your weekend trip — popular spots book out quickly. And ask your Sea & City Rentals host for their personal favorites — always the most useful food intel you'll get.
