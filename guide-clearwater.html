Where to Eat

Best Restaurants in the Area

Iconic Beach Bar
Frenchy's Rockaway Grill
Clearwater Beach institution since 1981. Fresh grouper sandwiches, cold beer, and feet-in-the-sand dining.
📍 7 Rockaway St, Clearwater Beach
Waterfront Seafood
The Salty Pelican
Relaxed waterfront bar with excellent fresh seafood and gorgeous sunset views over the Intracoastal.
📍 Georgia Ave, Indian Rocks Beach
Seafood Landmark
Crabby Bill's
A Gulf Coast tradition — fresh local seafood, stone crab claws in season, and ice cold beer on the water.
📍 401 Gulf Blvd, Indian Rocks Beach
Beachfront Dining
Palm Pavilion
Clearwater Beach's oldest beach bar (1926) with a full menu, frozen drinks, and you can't get closer to the sand.
📍 10 Bay Esplanade, Clearwater Beach
Italian on the Beach
Sea-Guini
Upscale Italian right on Clearwater Beach — housemade pasta, fresh seafood, and a romantic atmosphere.
📍 483 Mandalay Ave
Beach Casual
Clear Sky Cafe
Beloved local spot with a great breakfast, fresh seafood, and a wraparound porch perfect for people-watching.
📍 490 Mandalay Ave, Clearwater Beach

🦞 Local Tip

Stone crab season runs October through May — don't miss it. Crabby Bill's and The Salty Pelican serve them fresh. For the ultimate Clearwater experience, grab a grouper sandwich from Frenchy's and eat it with your feet in the sand.

The Beaches

Your Gulf Coast Playground

#1 Rated US Beach
Clearwater Beach
Powder-white sand, warm clear Gulf water, Pier 60, and a lively beachfront strip. Consistently America's top beach.
📍 Clearwater Beach island
Local Favorite
Indian Rocks Beach
Quieter, more residential feel with gorgeous sunsets, walkable dining, and a relaxed Old Florida vibe. Our home turf!
📍 Gulf Blvd, Indian Rocks Beach
Hidden Gem
Indian Shores
Less crowded neighbor to IRB with beautiful wide beach and great shelling. Locals' favorite secret.
📍 Gulf Blvd, Indian Shores
Pristine Wilderness
Caladesi Island
Accessible by ferry only — one of Florida's last untouched barrier islands with crystal water and no crowds.
Ferry from Honeymoon Island
State Park Beach
Honeymoon Island
Gorgeous state park with nature trails, osprey nests, a dog beach, and beautiful Gulf swimming.
📍 #1 Causeway Blvd, Dunedin
Local Beach — Free Parking
Sand Key Park
Beautiful county park beach with free parking (rare!), calm water, and a much more local crowd.
📍 1060 Gulf Blvd

🌅 Sunset Tip

Pier 60 at Clearwater Beach hosts a nightly sunset celebration with street performers, artisans, and musicians — it starts 2 hours before sunset and is completely free. Indian Rocks Beach sunsets are equally stunning with far fewer crowds.

🐬 Local Tip

Dolphins are everywhere in the Gulf and Intracoastal — you will likely spot them from the beach or on any boat tour. For the best chance, take a morning kayak on the Intracoastal behind Indian Rocks Beach around sunrise. They are incredibly active in the early hours.
